What MyBibleLens™ Is — and What It Isn't

MyBibleLens™ is an interpretive aid and subjective exploration tool for personal scripture study and reflection. It is designed to help individuals engage with Scripture through multiple lenses (Logos, Refiner, Etymology, Neuroscience, Philosopher, Dreams) and to support a personal practice of reflection, study, and growth.

MyBibleLens™ is not a substitute for Holy Scripture itself, official church doctrine, ordained pastoral counsel, theological education, mental-health care, medical advice, or any professional service. AI outputs are assistive and exploratory, not authoritative. Users are responsible for vetting AI-generated responses against official scriptural texts and qualified human guidance before sharing or acting upon them.

Doctrinal Neutrality

MyBibleLens™ is a Christian-rooted study tool that does not represent, endorse, or align with any specific Christian denomination, communion, or theological tradition. Branding terminology used within the product (such as "Apostle," "Sanctuary," "Kingdom," or "Apostolic Profile") is product nomenclature only and is not intended to convey denominational identity, sacramental authority, or formal religious office.

The purpose of the app, for you, is to support understanding of yourself — to step away from the chaos of social media and outside noise, to engage Scripture with curiosity, to feel more at home in church and Bible studies, and to make scripture study an active, creative practice rather than a passive one.   • Whether you intend to publish a write-up after disclosure, and if so, your preferred timeline.

3. What We Ask

  • Test against your own account only. Do not access, modify, or delete other users' data.
  • Avoid disruption. No denial-of-service testing, automated mass scanning, or resource-exhaustion attacks.
  • Do not exfiltrate. If you incidentally encounter user data, stop testing and report it immediately.
  • Reasonable disclosure window. Please give us a reasonable opportunity to investigate and remediate before any public disclosure (typically 90 days, unless otherwise agreed).
  • Comply with the law. All testing must comply with applicable computer-misuse laws (CFAA and equivalents).

4. Safe Harbor

MBL will not pursue legal action against good-faith security research that adheres to this policy. Accidental, good-faith violations of this policy that you report promptly will not be the basis for legal action by MBL. This safe-harbor commitment is offered by MBL only and does not bind third parties (such as our infrastructure providers); we encourage you to also review the policies of any affected third parties.

5. Acknowledgment & Recognition

We aim to acknowledge security reports within 24–48 hours and to provide an initial triage response within 5 business days, though response times may be extended during high-volume periods or holidays. 6. Out of Scope

  • Issues affecting only outdated browsers or unsupported software versions.
  • Self-XSS that requires the victim to paste payloads into their own console.
  • Findings that rely on social engineering of MBL personnel.
  • Theoretical vulnerabilities without demonstrable impact.
